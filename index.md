## About Me

[cite_start]I am currently an Assistant Researcher at the Cryptography Laboratory of Ant Group Technical Research Institute[cite: 7, 12, 17]. [cite_start]I received my Ph.D. degree in Cryptography from the School of Cyber Science and Technology at Shandong University, where I was advised by Prof. Meiqin Wang[cite: 8, 17]. [cite_start]Prior to that, I received my Bachelor's degree in Network Engineering from the School of Information and Communication Engineering at the University of Electronic Science and Technology of China (UESTC)[cite: 9, 16].

[cite_start]My research primarily focuses on Fully Homomorphic Encryption (FHE) and symmetric cryptography security analysis. [cite_start]I have extensive experience in industrial cryptography research, aiming to break through the efficiency bottlenecks of privacy-preserving computation in real-world scenarios and transform cutting-edge theories into high-value commercial solutions.

## Research Interests

- [cite_start]**Fully Homomorphic Encryption (FHE) & Privacy-Preserving Computation:** Deeply involved in the underlying optimization of encrypted machine learning, including addressing neural network inference latency and ciphertext expansion issues[cite: 19].
- [cite_start]**Symmetric Cryptography Analysis:** Focused on FHE-friendly symmetric ciphers and transciphering architectures[cite: 17, 19].
- [cite_start]**Cryptographic Engineering:** Strong capabilities in engineering and code implementation, including designing white-box protection schemes for financial-grade environments[cite: 20].
